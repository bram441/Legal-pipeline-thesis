A person is an alien if the person does not provide proof that the person possesses Belgian nationality.

A person is a third-country national if the person is not a Union citizen and the person is not a person enjoying the Community right of free movement as defined in Article 2, point 5, of the Schengen Borders Code.

A person has illegal stay if the person is an alien and the person does not meet or no longer meets the conditions for entry to or stay on the territory.

A person is an identified alien if the person is an alien and the person is in possession of a valid travel document, a valid passport, or a valid identity document.

A person is an identified alien if the person is an alien and the person has been recognised as a national by the national authority of their country and that national authority has declared itself willing to issue a laissez-passer.

A person is an identified alien if the person is an alien and the person falls within the category of nationalities for which the minister may personally issue a laissez-passer.

A risk of absconding exists for an alien if the alien is the subject of a removal procedure, a procedure for the granting of international protection, or a procedure for determining or transferring to the Member State responsible for examining the application for international protection, and there are reasons to believe that the alien will abscond, having regard to the criteria listed in paragraph 2.

A risk of absconding must be current and real.

A risk of absconding is established following an individual examination and on the basis of one or more objective criteria listed in paragraph 2, taking into account all circumstances specific to each case.

The absconding criterion under paragraph 2(1) is met for a person if the person did not submit a residence application following their illegal entry or during their illegal stay, or did not make their application for international protection within the time limit provided for by this Act.

The absconding criterion under paragraph 2(2) is met for a person if the person used false or misleading information or false or falsified documents in the context of a procedure for international protection, residence, removal, or refusal of entry, or committed fraud or used other unlawful means in such a procedure.

The absconding criterion under paragraph 2(3) is met for a person if the person does not cooperate or has not cooperated in the context of their relations with the authorities responsible for the implementation of or supervision of compliance with the regulations concerning access to the territory, residence, establishment, and removal of aliens.

The absconding criterion under paragraph 2(4) is met for a person if the person has made clear that they do not wish to comply with, or has already failed to comply with, a transfer, refusal of entry, or removal measure.

The absconding criterion under paragraph 2(4) is met for a person if the person has made clear that they do not wish to comply with, or has already failed to comply with, an entry ban that has neither been lifted nor suspended.

The absconding criterion under paragraph 2(4) is met for a person if the person has made clear that they do not wish to comply with, or has already failed to comply with, a less coercive measure than a deprivation of liberty measure aimed at guaranteeing their transfer, refusal of entry, or removal, regardless of whether it is a liberty-restricting measure or another measure.

The absconding criterion under paragraph 2(4) is met for a person if the person has made clear that they do not wish to comply with, or has already failed to comply with, a liberty-restricting measure aimed at guaranteeing public order or national security.

The absconding criterion under paragraph 2(4) is met for a person if the person has made clear that they do not wish to comply with, or has already failed to comply with, a measure taken by another Member State equivalent to a transfer measure, a refusal of entry measure, a removal measure, an entry ban, a less coercive measure, or a liberty-restricting measure as referred to in paragraph 2(4)(a) through (d).

The absconding criterion under paragraph 2(5) is met for a person if the person is the subject of an entry ban in the Kingdom or in another Member State that has neither been lifted nor suspended.

The absconding criterion under paragraph 2(6) is met for a person if the person submitted a new residence application or a new application for international protection immediately after having been the subject of a decision refusing entry or residence, a decision terminating their stay, or a refusal of entry or removal measure.

The absconding criterion under paragraph 2(7) is met for a person if the person, while being questioned on that point, concealed the fact that they had already provided their fingerprints in another State bound by the European regulations concerning the determination of the State responsible for examining an application for international protection, after having submitted an application for international protection.

The absconding criterion under paragraph 2(8) is met for a person if the person submitted multiple applications for international protection or multiple residence applications in the Kingdom or in one or more other Member States, and those applications gave rise to a negative decision or did not result in the issuance of a residence permit.

The absconding criterion under paragraph 2(9) is met for a person if the person, while being questioned on that point, concealed the fact that they had previously submitted an application for international protection in another State bound by the European regulations concerning the determination of the State responsible for examining an application for international protection.

The absconding criterion under paragraph 2(10) is met for a person if the person declared, or it appears from their file, that they came to the Kingdom for purposes other than those for which they submitted an application for international protection or a residence application.

The absconding criterion under paragraph 2(11) is met for a person if the person is the subject of a fine because they submitted a manifestly unlawful appeal to the Council for Alien Law Litigation.
