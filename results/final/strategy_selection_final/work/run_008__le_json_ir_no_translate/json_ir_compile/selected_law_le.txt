A person is a foreigner if the person does not provide proof that the person holds Belgian nationality.

A person is a third-country national if the person is not a Union citizen and the person is not a person who falls under Community law on free movement as defined in Article 2, point 5, of the Schengen Borders Code.

A situation constitutes illegal residence if a foreigner is present on the territory and the foreigner does not or no longer satisfies the conditions for access to or residence on the territory.

A risk of absconding exists in relation to a foreigner if there are reasons to believe that the foreigner, who is subject to a removal procedure, a procedure for granting international protection, or a procedure for determining or transferring to the Member State responsible for examining the request for international protection, will abscond, having regard to the criteria listed in paragraph 2.

A foreigner is an identified foreigner if the foreigner is in possession of a valid travel document, a valid passport, or a valid identity document.

A foreigner is an identified foreigner if the foreigner has been recognised as a national by the national authority of the foreigner's country and that authority has declared itself willing to issue a laissez-passer.

A foreigner is an identified foreigner if the foreigner falls under a nationality category for which the minister may issue a laissez-passer directly.

The risk of absconding must be current and real.

The risk of absconding is established after an individual examination and on the basis of one or more objective criteria, taking into account all circumstances specific to each case.

Objective criterion 1 is present if the person did not submit a residence application after illegal entry or during illegal residence, or did not submit a request for international protection within the period provided by the law.

Objective criterion 2 is present if the person used false or misleading information, false or falsified documents, committed fraud, or used other unlawful means in the context of a procedure for international protection, residence, removal, or pushback.

Objective criterion 3 is present if the person does not cooperate or has not cooperated in the context of relations with the authorities responsible for the implementation of and/or supervision of compliance with the regulations on access to the territory, residence, establishment, and removal of foreigners.

Objective criterion 4 is present if the person has indicated that the person does not wish to comply with one of the measures referred to in criterion 4, or has already failed to comply with one of those measures.

A measure referred to in criterion 4 is a transfer, pushback, or removal measure.

A measure referred to in criterion 4 is an entry ban that has neither been lifted nor suspended.

A measure referred to in criterion 4 is a less coercive measure than a deprivation of liberty measure aimed at guaranteeing the person's transfer, pushback, or removal, whether a freedom-restricting measure or another measure.

A measure referred to in criterion 4 is a freedom-restricting measure aimed at guaranteeing public order or national security.

A measure referred to in criterion 4 is a measure taken by another Member State that is equivalent to a measure referred to in criterion 4.

Objective criterion 5 is present if the person is subject to an entry ban in the Kingdom and/or in another Member State that has neither been lifted nor suspended.

Objective criterion 6 is present if the person submitted a new residence application or a new request for international protection immediately after having been subject to a decision refusing entry or residence, or a decision ending the person's residence, or immediately after having been subject to a pushback or removal measure.

Objective criterion 7 is present if the person, while being questioned on that point, concealed that the person had already provided fingerprints in another State bound by the European regulations on determining the State responsible for examining a request for international protection, after having submitted a request for international protection.

Objective criterion 8 is present if the person has submitted multiple requests for international protection and/or residence applications in the Kingdom or in one or more other Member States, which have resulted in a negative decision or have not led to the issuance of a residence permit.

Objective criterion 9 is present if the person, while being questioned on that point, concealed that the person had previously submitted a request for international protection in another State bound by the European regulations on determining the State responsible for examining a request for international protection.

Objective criterion 10 is present if the person declared, or if it appears from the person's file, that the person came to the Kingdom for purposes other than the purposes for which the person submitted a request for international protection or a residence application.

Objective criterion 11 is present if the person is subject to a fine because the person submitted a manifestly unlawful appeal to the Council for Alien Law Litigation.
