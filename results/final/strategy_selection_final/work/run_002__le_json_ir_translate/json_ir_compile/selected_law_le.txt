Article 1, paragraph 1, point 1.
A person is an alien if the person does not provide proof that the person possesses Belgian nationality.

Article 1, paragraph 1, point 3.
A person is a third-country national if the person is not a Union citizen and the person is not a person enjoying the Community right of free movement as defined in Article 2, point 5, of the Schengen Borders Code.

Article 1, paragraph 1, point 4.
An alien has an illegal stay if the alien is present on the territory and the alien does not meet or no longer meets the conditions for entry to the territory or stay on the territory.

Article 1, paragraph 1, point 14, subpoint a.
An alien is an identified alien if the alien is in possession of a valid travel document, a valid passport, or a valid identity document.

Article 1, paragraph 1, point 14, subpoint b.
An alien is an identified alien if the alien has been recognised as a national by the national authority of the alien's country and that national authority has declared itself willing to issue a laissez-passer.

Article 1, paragraph 1, point 14, subpoint c.
An alien is an identified alien if the alien falls within the category of nationalities for which the minister may personally issue a laissez-passer.

Article 1, paragraph 1, point 11.
A risk of absconding exists for an alien if the alien is the subject of a removal procedure, a procedure for the granting of international protection, or a procedure for determining or transferring to the Member State responsible for examining the application for international protection, and there are reasons to believe that the alien will abscond.

Article 1, paragraph 2, general.
A risk of absconding must be current and real.

Article 1, paragraph 2, general.
A risk of absconding is established following an individual examination and on the basis of one or more objective criteria listed in Article 1, paragraph 2, taking into account all circumstances specific to each case.

Article 1, paragraph 2, point 1.
The absconding criterion under point 1 is met for a person if the person did not submit a residence application following illegal entry or during illegal stay, or did not make an application for international protection within the time limit provided for by this Act.

Article 1, paragraph 2, point 2.
The absconding criterion under point 2 is met for a person if the person used false or misleading information or false or falsified documents in the context of a procedure for international protection, residence, removal, or refusal of entry, or committed fraud or used other unlawful means in such a procedure.

Article 1, paragraph 2, point 3.
The absconding criterion under point 3 is met for a person if the person does not cooperate or has not cooperated in the context of relations with the authorities responsible for the implementation of or supervision of compliance with the regulations concerning access to the territory, residence, establishment, and removal of aliens.

Article 1, paragraph 2, point 4, subpoint a.
The absconding criterion under point 4 is met for a person if the person has made clear that they do not wish to comply with, or has already failed to comply with, a transfer measure, a refusal of entry measure, or a removal measure.

Article 1, paragraph 2, point 4, subpoint b.
The absconding criterion under point 4 is met for a person if the person has made clear that they do not wish to comply with, or has already failed to comply with, an entry ban that has neither been lifted nor suspended.

Article 1, paragraph 2, point 4, subpoint c.
The absconding criterion under point 4 is met for a person if the person has made clear that they do not wish to comply with, or has already failed to comply with, a measure less coercive than a deprivation of liberty measure aimed at guaranteeing the person's transfer, refusal of entry, or removal, regardless of whether it is a liberty-restricting measure or another measure.

Article 1, paragraph 2, point 4, subpoint d.
The absconding criterion under point 4 is met for a person if the person has made clear that they do not wish to comply with, or has already failed to comply with, a liberty-restricting measure aimed at guaranteeing public order or national security.

Article 1, paragraph 2, point 4, subpoint e.
The absconding criterion under point 4 is met for a person if the person has made clear that they do not wish to comply with, or has already failed to comply with, a measure taken by another Member State that is equivalent to a measure referred to in Article 1, paragraph 2, point 4, subpoints a, b, c, or d.

Article 1, paragraph 2, point 5.
The absconding criterion under point 5 is met for a person if the person is the subject of an entry ban in the Kingdom, or in another Member State, that has neither been lifted nor suspended.

Article 1, paragraph 2, point 6.
The absconding criterion under point 6 is met for a person if the person submitted a new residence application or a new application for international protection immediately after having been the subject of a decision refusing entry or residence, a decision terminating stay, or a refusal of entry or removal measure.

Article 1, paragraph 2, point 7.
The absconding criterion under point 7 is met for a person if, while being questioned on that point, the person concealed the fact that the person had already provided fingerprints in another State bound by the European regulations concerning the determination of the State responsible for examining an application for international protection, after having submitted an application for international protection.

Article 1, paragraph 2, point 8.
The absconding criterion under point 8 is met for a person if the person submitted multiple applications for international protection or multiple residence applications in the Kingdom or in one or more other Member States, and those applications gave rise to a negative decision or did not result in the issuance of a residence permit.

Article 1, paragraph 2, point 9.
The absconding criterion under point 9 is met for a person if, while being questioned on that point, the person concealed the fact that the person had previously submitted an application for international protection in another State bound by the European regulations concerning the determination of the State responsible for examining an application for international protection.

Article 1, paragraph 2, point 10.
The absconding criterion under point 10 is met for a person if the person declared, or it appears from the person's file, that the person came to the Kingdom for purposes other than those for which the person submitted an application for international protection or a residence application.

Article 1, paragraph 2, point 11.
The absconding criterion under point 11 is met for a person if the person is the subject of a fine because the person submitted a manifestly unlawful appeal to the Council for Alien Law Litigation.
