Art. 4.17 par. 1.
The surviving spouse acquires usufruct of the entire estate if the deceased leaves descendants, adopted children, or descendants of adopted children.

Art. 4.17 par. 2.
The surviving spouse acquires full ownership of the share of the first-dying spouse in the common property if the deceased leaves relatives in the ascending line or close collateral relatives.

Art. 4.17 par. 2.
The surviving spouse acquires full ownership of the share of the first-dying spouse in the property held exclusively in undivided ownership between the spouses if the deceased leaves relatives in the ascending line or close collateral relatives.

Art. 4.17 par. 2.
The surviving spouse acquires usufruct of the remaining goods of the own property of the deceased if the deceased leaves relatives in the ascending line or close collateral relatives.

Art. 4.17 par. 2 (3).
The surviving spouse acquires usufruct of goods subject to the right of legal return referred to in article 4.24 if the deceased leaves relatives in the ascending line or close collateral relatives.

Art. 4.17 par. 2 (3).
The surviving spouse acquires usufruct of goods subject to the right of legal return referred to in article 4.25 if the deceased leaves relatives in the ascending line or close collateral relatives.

Art. 4.17 par. 2 (3).
The surviving spouse does not acquire usufruct of goods subject to the right of legal return if the deed of gift provides otherwise.

Art. 4.17 par. 2 (3).
The surviving spouse does not acquire usufruct of goods subject to the right of legal return if the testament provides otherwise.

Art. 4.17 par. 3.
The surviving spouse acquires full ownership of the entire estate if the deceased leaves other heirs than those referred to in article 4.17 par. 1 and article 4.17 par. 2.

Art. 4.17 par. 3.
The surviving spouse acquires full ownership of the entire estate if the deceased leaves no heirs.
