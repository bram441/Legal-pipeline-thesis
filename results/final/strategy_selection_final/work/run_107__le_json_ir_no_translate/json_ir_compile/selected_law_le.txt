A person is a foreigner if that person does not provide proof that they hold Belgian nationality.

A person is a national of a third country if that person is not a citizen of the Union and that person is not a person who falls under Community law on free movement as defined in Article 2, point 5, of the Schengen Borders Code.

A person is in illegal residence if that person is present on the territory and does not or no longer satisfies the conditions for access to or residence on the territory.

A risk of absconding exists for a foreigner if there are reasons to believe that the foreigner, who is subject to a removal procedure, a procedure for granting international protection, or a procedure for determining or transferring to the Member State responsible for examining the application for international protection, will abscond, having regard to the criteria listed in paragraph 2.

A risk of absconding must be current and real.

A risk of absconding is established after an individual examination and on the basis of one or more objective criteria listed in paragraph 2, taking into account all circumstances specific to each case.

A foreigner is an identified foreigner if that foreigner is in possession of a valid travel document, a valid passport, or a valid identity document.

A foreigner is an identified foreigner if that foreigner has been recognised as a national by the national authority of their country, which has declared itself willing to issue a laissez-passer.

A foreigner is an identified foreigner if that foreigner falls under a category of nationalities for which the minister may issue a laissez-passer directly.

The absconding criterion 1 is met for a foreigner if that foreigner did not submit a residence application after illegal entry or during illegal residence, or did not make an application for international protection within the period provided by this law.

The absconding criterion 2 is met for a foreigner if that foreigner used false or misleading information or false or falsified documents in the context of a procedure for international protection, residence, removal, or pushback, or committed fraud or used other unlawful means.

The absconding criterion 3 is met for a foreigner if that foreigner does not cooperate or has not cooperated in the context of their relations with the authorities responsible for implementing and/or supervising compliance with the regulations on access to the territory, residence, establishment, and removal of foreigners.

The absconding criterion 4 is met for a foreigner if that foreigner has clearly indicated that they do not wish to comply with, or has already failed to comply with, a transfer, pushback, or removal measure.

The absconding criterion 4 is met for a foreigner if that foreigner has clearly indicated that they do not wish to comply with, or has already failed to comply with, an entry ban that has neither been lifted nor suspended.

The absconding criterion 4 is met for a foreigner if that foreigner has clearly indicated that they do not wish to comply with, or has already failed to comply with, a less coercive measure than a deprivation of liberty measure aimed at guaranteeing their transfer, pushback, or removal, whether a freedom-restricting measure or another measure.

The absconding criterion 4 is met for a foreigner if that foreigner has clearly indicated that they do not wish to comply with, or has already failed to comply with, a freedom-restricting measure aimed at guaranteeing public order or national security.

The absconding criterion 4 is met for a foreigner if that foreigner has clearly indicated that they do not wish to comply with, or has already failed to comply with, a measure taken by another Member State that is equivalent to the measures referred to in criterion 4(a), (b), (c), or (d).

The absconding criterion 5 is met for a foreigner if that foreigner is subject to an entry ban in the Kingdom and/or in another Member State that has neither been lifted nor suspended.

The absconding criterion 6 is met for a foreigner if that foreigner submitted a new residence application or a new application for international protection immediately after being subject to a decision refusing entry or residence, or a decision ending their residence, or immediately after being subject to a pushback or removal measure.

The absconding criterion 7 is met for a foreigner if that foreigner, while being questioned on that point, concealed that they had already provided fingerprints in another State bound by the European regulations on determining the State responsible for examining an application for international protection, after having submitted an application for international protection.

The absconding criterion 8 is met for a foreigner if that foreigner has submitted multiple applications for international protection and/or residence applications in the Kingdom or in one or more other Member States, which resulted in a negative decision or did not lead to the issuance of a residence permit.

The absconding criterion 9 is met for a foreigner if that foreigner, while being questioned on that point, concealed that they had previously submitted an application for international protection in another State bound by the European regulations on determining the State responsible for examining an application for international protection.

The absconding criterion 10 is met for a foreigner if that foreigner declared, or if it appears from their file, that they came to the Kingdom for purposes other than those for which they submitted an application for international protection or a residence application.

The absconding criterion 11 is met for a foreigner if that foreigner is subject to a fine because they submitted a manifestly unlawful appeal to the Council for Alien Law Litigation.
