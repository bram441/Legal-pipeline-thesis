par. 1, point 1: An alien is any person who does not provide proof that they possess Belgian nationality.

par. 1, point 3: A third-country national is any person who is not a Union citizen and who is not a person enjoying the Community right of free movement as defined in Article 2, point 5, of the Schengen Borders Code.

par. 1, point 4: An illegal stay is the presence on the territory of an alien who does not or no longer meets the conditions for entry to or stay on the territory.

par. 1, point 11: A risk of absconding exists for an alien if there are reasons to believe that the alien will abscond, having regard to the criteria listed in paragraph 2.

par. 1, point 11: A risk of absconding may exist only if the alien is the subject of a removal procedure, a procedure for the granting of international protection, or a procedure for the determination of or transfer to the Member State responsible for examining the application for international protection.

par. 1, point 14(a): An alien is an identified alien if the alien is in possession of a valid travel document, a valid passport, or a valid identity document.

par. 1, point 14(b): An alien is an identified alien if the alien has been recognised as a national by the national authority of their country and that authority has declared itself willing to issue a laissez-passer.

par. 1, point 14(c): An alien is an identified alien if the alien falls within the category of nationalities for which the minister may personally issue a laissez-passer.

par. 2, opening: A risk of absconding must be current and real.

par. 2, opening: A risk of absconding is established following an individual examination and on the basis of one or more objective criteria, taking into account all circumstances specific to each case.

par. 2, point 1: The absconding criterion 1 is met for a person if the person did not submit a residence application following their illegal entry or during their illegal stay.

par. 2, point 1: The absconding criterion 1 is met for a person if the person did not make their application for international protection within the time limit provided for by this Act.

par. 2, point 2: The absconding criterion 2 is met for a person if the person used false or misleading information in the context of a procedure for international protection, residence, removal, or refusal of entry.

par. 2, point 2: The absconding criterion 2 is met for a person if the person used false or falsified documents in the context of a procedure for international protection, residence, removal, or refusal of entry.

par. 2, point 2: The absconding criterion 2 is met for a person if the person committed fraud or used other unlawful means in the context of a procedure for international protection, residence, removal, or refusal of entry.

par. 2, point 3: The absconding criterion 3 is met for a person if the person does not cooperate or has not cooperated in the context of their relations with the authorities responsible for the implementation of or supervision of compliance with the regulations concerning access to the territory, residence, establishment, and removal of aliens.

par. 2, point 4(a): The absconding criterion 4 is met for a person if the person has made clear that they do not wish to comply with, or has already failed to comply with, a transfer, refusal of entry, or removal measure.

par. 2, point 4(b): The absconding criterion 4 is met for a person if the person has made clear that they do not wish to comply with, or has already failed to comply with, an entry ban that has neither been lifted nor suspended.

par. 2, point 4(c): The absconding criterion 4 is met for a person if the person has made clear that they do not wish to comply with, or has already failed to comply with, a less coercive measure than a deprivation of liberty measure aimed at guaranteeing their transfer, refusal of entry, or removal, regardless of whether it is a liberty-restricting measure or another measure.

par. 2, point 4(d): The absconding criterion 4 is met for a person if the person has made clear that they do not wish to comply with, or has already failed to comply with, a liberty-restricting measure aimed at guaranteeing public order or national security.

par. 2, point 4(e): The absconding criterion 4 is met for a person if the person has made clear that they do not wish to comply with, or has already failed to comply with, a measure taken by another Member State equivalent to a transfer measure, a refusal of entry measure, a removal measure, an entry ban, a less coercive measure, or a liberty-restricting measure as referred to in points 4(a) through 4(d).

par. 2, point 5: The absconding criterion 5 is met for a person if the person is the subject of an entry ban in the Kingdom that has neither been lifted nor suspended.

par. 2, point 5: The absconding criterion 5 is met for a person if the person is the subject of an entry ban in another Member State that has neither been lifted nor suspended.

par. 2, point 6: The absconding criterion 6 is met for a person if the person submitted a new residence application or a new application for international protection immediately after having been the subject of a decision refusing entry or residence.

par. 2, point 6: The absconding criterion 6 is met for a person if the person submitted a new residence application or a new application for international protection immediately after having been the subject of a decision terminating their stay.

par. 2, point 6: The absconding criterion 6 is met for a person if the person submitted a new residence application or a new application for international protection immediately after having been the subject of a refusal of entry or removal measure.

par. 2, point 7: The absconding criterion 7 is met for a person if, while being questioned in connection with that point, the person concealed the fact that they had already provided their fingerprints in another State bound by the European regulations concerning the determination of the State responsible for examining an application for international protection, after having submitted an application for international protection.

par. 2, point 8: The absconding criterion 8 is met for a person if the person has submitted multiple applications for international protection in the Kingdom or in one or more other Member States that have given rise to a negative decision or that have not resulted in the issuance of a residence permit.

par. 2, point 8: The absconding criterion 8 is met for a person if the person has submitted multiple residence applications in the Kingdom or in one or more other Member States that have given rise to a negative decision or that have not resulted in the issuance of a residence permit.

par. 2, point 9: The absconding criterion 9 is met for a person if, while being questioned in connection with that point, the person concealed the fact that they had previously already submitted an application for international protection in another State bound by the European regulations concerning the determination of the State responsible for examining an application for international protection.

par. 2, point 10: The absconding criterion 10 is met for a person if the person has declared that they came to the Kingdom for purposes other than those for which they submitted an application for international protection or a residence application.

par. 2, point 10: The absconding criterion 10 is met for a person if it appears from the person's file that they came to the Kingdom for purposes other than those for which they submitted an application for international protection or a residence application.

par. 2, point 11: The absconding criterion 11 is met for a person if the person is the subject of a fine because they submitted a manifestly unlawful appeal to the Council for Alien Law Litigation.
