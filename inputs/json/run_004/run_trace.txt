SECTION II. - INTENTIONAL KILLING, NOT CALLED MANSLAUGHTER, AND INTENTIONAL INFLICTING OF BODILY INJURY.

  Art. 398. Whoever intentionally inflicts wounds or blows shall be punished with imprisonment from eight days to six months and a fine from twenty-six [euros] to one hundred [euros], or with one of these penalties alone.
  In case the offender acted with premeditation, he shall be sentenced to imprisonment from one month to one year and a fine from fifty [euros] to two hundred [euros].

  Art. 399. If the blows or wounds result in illness or incapacity to perform personal labor, the offender shall be punished with imprisonment from two months to two years and a fine from fifty [euros] to two hundred [euros].
  The offender shall be punished with imprisonment from six months to three years and a fine from one hundred [euros] to five hundred [euros], if he acted with premeditation.

  Art. 400. The penalty shall be imprisonment from two years to five years and a fine from two hundred [euros] to five hundred [euros], if the blows or wounds result in an apparently incurable illness, incapacity to perform personal labor for more than four months, total loss of the use of an organ, or a severe mutilation.
  The penalty shall be imprisonment from five years to ten years, in case the offender acted with premeditation.

  Art. 401. When the blows or wounds are intentionally inflicted, but without the intent to kill, and yet cause death, the offender shall be punished with imprisonment from five years to ten years.
  He shall be punished with imprisonment from ten years to fifteen years if he commits these violent acts with premeditation.
