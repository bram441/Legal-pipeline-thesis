SECTION II. - INTENTIONAL KILLING, NOT CALLED MANSLAUGHTER, AND INTENTIONAL INFLICTING OF BODILY INJURY.

Art. 398. Whoever intentionally inflicts wounds or blows shall be punished with imprisonment from eight days to six months and with a fine of twenty-six [euros] to one hundred [euros] or with one of these punishments alone.  
If the offender acted with premeditation, he shall be sentenced to imprisonment from one month to one year and to a fine of fifty [euros] to two hundred [euros].

Art. 399. If the blows or wounds cause an illness or incapacity to perform personal labor, the offender shall be punished with imprisonment from two months to two years and with a fine of fifty [euros] to two hundred [euros].  
The offender shall be punished with imprisonment from six months to three years and with a fine of one hundred [euros] to five hundred [euros], if he acted with premeditation.

Art. 400. The punishment is imprisonment from two years to five years and a fine of two hundred [euros] to five hundred [euros], if the blows or wounds cause an incurable seeming disease, incapacity to perform personal labor for more than four months, complete loss of use of an organ, or a severe mutilation.  
The penalty is imprisonment from five years to ten years, in case the offender acted with premeditation.

Art. 401. When the blows or wounds are intentionally inflicted, but without the purpose of killing, and yet cause death, the offender shall be punished with imprisonment from five years to ten years.  
He shall be punished with imprisonment from ten years to fifteen years if he commits those acts of violence with premeditation.
