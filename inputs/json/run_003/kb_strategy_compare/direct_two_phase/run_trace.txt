SECTION II. - INTENTIONAL KILLING, NOT CALLED MANSLAUGHTER, AND INTENTIONAL INFLICTING OF BODILY INJURY.

Art. 398. Whoever intentionally inflicts injuries or blows shall be punished with imprisonment from eight days to six months and a fine from twenty-six [euro] to one hundred [euro], or with one of these penalties alone.
If the offender acted with premeditation, he shall be sentenced to imprisonment from one month to one year and a fine from fifty [euro] to two hundred [euro].

Art. 399. If the blows or injuries cause illness or incapacity to perform personal labor, the offender shall be punished with imprisonment from two months to two years and a fine from fifty [euro] to two hundred [euro].
The offender shall be punished with imprisonment from six months to three years and a fine from one hundred [euro] to five hundred [euro], if he acted with premeditation.

Art. 400. The penalty is imprisonment from two years to five years and a fine from two hundred [euro] to five hundred [euro], if the blows or injuries result in an incurable seeming disease, incapacity to perform personal labor for more than four months, complete loss of use of an organ, or a severe mutilation.
The penalty is detention from five years to ten years if the offender acted with premeditation.

Art. 401. When the blows or injuries are intentionally inflicted, but without the aim to kill, and yet cause death, the offender shall be punished with detention from five years to ten years.
He shall be punished with detention from ten years to fifteen years if he commits those violent acts with premeditation.
