SECTION II. - INTENTIONAL KILLING, NOT CALLED MANSLAUGHTER, AND INTENTIONAL INFLICTING OF BODILY INJURY.

  Art. 398. Whoever intentionally causes injuries or blows shall be punished with imprisonment from eight days to six months and a fine from twenty-six [euros] to one hundred [euros] or with one of these penalties alone.
  In case the offender acted with premeditation, he shall be sentenced to imprisonment from one month to one year and a fine from fifty [euros] to two hundred [euros].

  Art. 399. If the blows or injuries cause an illness or incapacity to perform personal labor as a result, the offender shall be punished with imprisonment from two months to two years and a fine from fifty [euros] to two hundred [euros].
  The offender shall be punished with imprisonment from six months to three years and a fine from one hundred [euros] to five hundred [euros] if he acted with premeditation.

  Art. 400. The penalty is imprisonment from two years to five years and a fine from two hundred [euros] to five hundred [euros] if the blows or injuries result in an apparently incurable illness, incapacity to perform personal labor for more than four months, complete loss of use of an organ, or a severe maiming.
  The penalty is imprisonment from five years to ten years in case the offender acted with premeditation.

  Art. 401. When the blows or injuries are intentionally inflicted, but without the aim to kill, and still cause death, the offender shall be punished with imprisonment from five years to ten years.
  He shall be punished with imprisonment from ten years to fifteen years if he commits these violent acts with premeditation.
