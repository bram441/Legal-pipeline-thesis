 (4) illegal stay: presence on the territory of a foreigner who does not or no longer meets the conditions for access to or residence on the territory;
 (5) return: the fact that a national of a third country, either voluntarily after having been subject to a removal decision or forcibly, returns to their country of origin or a transit country pursuant to Community or bilateral readmission agreements, or to another third country to which the person concerned decides to return voluntarily and where they are authorized or admitted to stay;
 (6) removal decision: the decision establishing the illegality of a foreigner's stay and imposing an obligation to return;
 (7) removal: the enforcement of the removal decision, namely the physical removal from the territory;
 (8) [4 entry ban: the decision that may accompany a removal decision prohibiting access to and residence on the territory of the Kingdom or the territory of all Member States, including the territory of the Kingdom, for a specified period;]4
 (9) voluntary departure: leaving the territory within the period set for this in the removal decision;
 (10) voluntary return: the return of a person to their country of origin or a third country where they are admitted to stay on the territory, following an independently taken decision to use a return assistance program developed by the host country's government;
 (11) [5 risk of absconding: the fact that there are reasons to believe that a foreigner subject to a removal procedure, a procedure for granting international protection, or a procedure to determine or transfer to the Member State responsible for handling the request for international protection, in view of the criteria listed in para. 2, will abscond.]5
 (12) vulnerable person: both accompanied and unaccompanied minors, persons with disabilities, elderly persons, pregnant women, single parents with minor children, and persons who have been tortured or raped or have suffered other serious forms of psychological, physical, or sexual violence;

 (10) the person concerned has declared or their file shows that they came to the Kingdom for purposes other than those for which they submitted a request for international protection or residence application;
 (11) the person concerned is subject to a fine for having submitted a manifestly unfounded appeal to the Immigration Appeals Board.]5
